A Vacation to Siwa Oasis

Last summer, I escaped to Siwa Oasis, a magical place tucked deep in Egypt’s Western Desert. As soon as I arrived, the air felt different calm and pure surrounded by thousands of date palms and olive trees fed by hundreds of natural springs. I spent mornings floating in Siwa’s salt lakes, where the salt concentration is so high it’s impossible to sink. Then I soaked in Cleopatra’s Spring and Bir Wahed, a bubbling hot pool tucked among the dunes. In the afternoons, I explored the ruins of the ancient Temple of Amun, once home to the famous oracle once visited by Alexander the Great. I also climbed up to the old Shali Fortress at sunset and was rewarded with a breathtaking view over the oasis’s sea of palms and desert beyond. At night, the desert sky exploded with stars. I stayed in a humble eco-lodge built from salt rock and clay, lit by the flicker of candles — completely unplugged. The peace, the history, and the raw natural beauty made Siwa feel like a dream.

Pros of going to Siwa Oasis

  • Unique Natural Springs & Therapeutic Waters

    • Siwa Oasis is home to numerous natural springs, including warm sulfur-rich ones, which are believed to help with skin conditions, joint ailments, and respiratory issues. Cleopatra’s Spring (also called Cleopatra’s Bath) offers a lush, relaxing swimming spot immersed in desert scenery.

  • Salt Lakes with Floating & Healing Properties

    • The salt lakes in Siwa are mineral-rich and have very high salinity, allowing for effortless floating—similar to the Dead Sea. These lakes also provide potential health benefits, such as skin detoxification and relief from certain ailments.

  • Rich Cultural and Archaeological Heritage

    • Siwa contains major historical sites such as the Temple of the Oracle of Amun, where Alexander the Great allegedly consulted the oracle. The ancient Shali Fortress, made from mud and salt, offers panoramic views across the palm groves and dunes.
